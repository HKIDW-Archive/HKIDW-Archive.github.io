  • Creative Place-making Ideas

    By Tai-O Cultural Festival design teams

    The workshop will build on the HKIDA’s experience of Tai-O Cultural Festival, a pilot scheme for the Characteristic Local Tourism by the Hong Kong Tourism Board, which addresses key principles to ensure the sustainability of cultural heritage while also finding a balance between local communities and tourists.

    Workshop participants will explore the value of local tourism promotion to cultural heritage and how interior and social design can be effectively used to build Hong Kong’s local characteristics.

  • Disassemble for Assemble – Transformation of Daily Objects for Spatial Design

    By 1/100 Academy

    1/100 Academy was established by a local architectural design firm Knock Knock Ideas. They believe the right to seek for good space design belongs to all of us, not just architects. For the sake of healthy development of our city, we need to learn how to observe like a designer, from the smallest details to the biggest monuments. And more importantly, to convert these observations into imaginations and thoughts. By introducing basic architectural design concepts and processes of thinking, the group wishes to inspire more people from different backgrounds to shift from “a user” to “a design team member”. Together we should be a team that construct genuine “spaces for people”.

    This workshop will discuss about the concept of “scale conversion”. Participants will be given materials to disassemble daily use objects, and reassemble them in a creative way with addition of accessories to become an interesting interior / exterior space.

  • Introduction to Chinese Woodwork Tools

    By Ken Chow

    Ken Chow is a largely self-taught woodworker. Having chosen to devote his whole life to carpentry and to perfect his craft through experimentation and innovation, after many apprenticeships he founded Yat Muk Studio to practise, research and teach woodwork full-time.

    In this hands-on workshop, let us explore different types and functions of Chinese woodworking tools such as planes, drills and chisels, and keeping the traditional Chinese Carpentry and Craftsmanship alive in Hong Kong. Each participant will make a piece of Walnut coaster as a souvenir.

  • Introduction to Chinese Wood Finishing Techniques

    By Ken Chow

    Ken Chow is a largely self-taught woodworker. Having chosen to devote his whole life to carpentry and to perfect his craft through experimentation and innovation, after many apprenticeships he founded Yat Muk Studio to practise, research and teach woodwork full-time.

    Alongside design, structure, ornaments, the original finishes applied define the particularities and value of traditional Chinese furniture. In this hands-on workshop, let us explore different types of Chinese wood finishes, such as lacquer, oil and wax. Each participant will make a piece of Walnut coaster as a souvenir.

  • Coffee Grounds Compost Planting

    By Eco-Greenergy

    Eco-Greenergy is an environmental social enterprise founded in 2014. Our core businesses include Zero Grounds Coffee Campaign (ZGCC), eco products retail sales & customization and eco education workshops. From daily operation to product selection and design, being eco and user-friendly is our core value. Offering employment/ practicum opportunities to underprivileged talent is also our mission to serve. With the simple and sustainable solutions, customers can go green with us easily while enhancing an inclusive society!

    The Coffee Grounds Ornament Planting Workshop is driven by the “Zero Grounds Coffee Campaign”. During the workshop, participants can learn and experience the properties, usage and recycling mechanism or coffee grounds together with the basic composting principles. The compost and planting pots arranged are all upcycled from coffee grounds locally recycled in Hong Kong. Each participant will finish 2 pots of ornament plants. With the natural color and texture of the coffee grounds, the pots serve as a perfect additive to the home furnishing.
